Description

Carbonara is a classic Italian pasta dish made with eggs, cheese, pancetta or guanciale, and black pepper.

Ingredients

  • 8 ounces spaghetti
  • 2 large eggs
  • ½ cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 4 slices bacon (or guanciale for authentic version)
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • Kosher salt and freshly ground black pepper
  • 2 tablespoons fresh parsley leaves (optional, for garnish)
  1. Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Cook spaghetti according to package instructions; reserve ½ cup of pasta water, then drain.
  2. In a small bowl, whisk together eggs and Parmesan until smooth; set aside.
  3. Heat a skillet over medium-high heat. Cook bacon until crispy, about 6–8 minutes. Remove excess fat, leaving about 1 tablespoon.
  4. Add garlic to the skillet and sauté until fragrant, about 1 minute. Reduce heat to low.
  5. Quickly add the drained pasta to the skillet. Pour in the egg-Parmesan mixture, tossing continuously to coat the pasta without scrambling the eggs.
  6. Add reserved pasta water, one tablespoon at a time, until the sauce reaches a creamy consistency.
  7. Season with salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste.
  8. Serve immediately, garnished with parsley and extra Parmesan if desired.
